FAQ

Why is this information important?

While it is easy to find campaign finance information, it is extremely difficult to make sense of that data. The Behind the Curtain app uses technology to help voters visualize campaign finance data in a more meaningful way. Ultimately, we believe voters need more approachable information about who is funding candidates for elected office.

Where did you get the data?

Campaign finance data is one of the most transparent public knowledge banks available thanks to federal and state reporting laws. All of our data comes directly from the Oregon Secretary of State's website.

Why have some campaigns spent more money than they have raised?

We calculate our "money raised" category based on how much money a campaign has raised during the current election cycle - so starting November 2012. It is not uncommon for candidates to have money remaining from previous campaigns that they can carry forward for future campaigns. Currently this amount is not accounted for in our totals. Therefore, campaigns may appear to have spent more than they have raised.

How current is your data?

The Oregon Secretary of State does not have the infrastructure set up to provide regular data dumps, therefore we must scrape the data directly from their website in order to keep our data as current as possible. We do this data scrape daily in an attempt to provide the most current information as possible. However, due to the structure of their site, our scrapers do occasionally miss some of the data. Each record generated on our site provides the number of transactions that our data is using to calculate our totals.
